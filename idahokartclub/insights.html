Mario Kart 101

Mario Kart is the name of a series of racing video games that are fun for all ages. The series is a spin-off of the Super Mario series which allows you to race as popular characters such as Mario, Luigi, Princess Peach, and Bowser. The series began with Super Mario Kart, which was released in 1992 on the Super Nintendo Entertainment System. The series currently has nine main entries, the latest being Mario Kart Tour, which is a mobile game. The popularity of the game comes from the fact that it is easy for anyone to pick up and have a good time while also having several technical aspects that make the game difficult to truly master.

While most of what we learn from Mario Kart comes from experience, sometimes a little hint can make a big difference. First thing's first, if you really want to get good at the game, learning drifting and getting mini-turbos is the most important skill to develop. Also, finding and learning how to use shortcuts can really help you get ahead of opponents who don't have that information. Remember to get lots of practice with game modes like Versus, Online, and especially Time Trials. Finally, if you're falling behind in the race, don't give up or panic! Remaining calm while racing can help with staying focused on making the right moves.

Weekly Trivia

  • In Mario Kart 8, there is an unused icon for a Spiny Shell Cup that would've been part of the game's DLC. The Spiny Shell Cup would eventually be revealed as being part of the Booster Course Pack DLC for Mario Kart 8 Deluxe. [Source]
  • In Mario Kart Wii, two of the game's new courses, Luigi Circuit and Mario Circuit, both use the same background song.
  • Mario Kart Tour is the first game in the series to feature alternate costumes for Bowser, including Meowser and the recently added Dr. Bowser.
  • Despite being the best characters, the Koopalings only appear in the two latest entries of Mario Kart.
